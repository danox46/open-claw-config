# Research Summary: LinkedIn Smoke Test — B2B AI Consulting for Mid-Market Operations Leaders

## 1. Campaign — Audience Pain Points, Competitive Positioning, Key Messages

### Primary Audience
- **Who:** Operations leaders (VP/Director level) at mid-market companies (100–1,000 employees, $10M–$100M revenue)
- **Context:** 78% of mid-market U.S. companies have adopted AI (up from 55% in 2024), but only 20% report scaling beyond pilots
- **Role:** Technologists, data scientists, and community leaders who are resource-constrained and time-poor (<10 hours/week for strategic tech decisions)

### Core Pain Points
| Priority | Pain Point | Evidence |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **High** | Data quality & siloed legacy systems | Gartner predicts 60% of AI projects fail due to lack of AI-ready data by 2027 |
| **High** | Scaling beyond pilots to production | 78% adoption but only 20% scale past pilot stage |
| **High** | Unclear ROI within 30–90 days | Mid-market buyers demand tangible outcomes within 30–90 days |
| **Medium** | Integration complexity | "Integration is prerequisite for innovation" (2026) |
| **Medium** | Lack of in-house AI expertise | Only 7% of operations leaders rate workforce as fully AI-ready |
| **Medium** | Security & governance concerns | 63% face cyber attacks; regulatory uncertainty around agentic AI |

### Competitive Positioning Opportunities
**Strong Angles:**
- **"Enterprise capabilities without enterprise complexity"** — Mid-market gap: enterprise solutions too complex, SMB solutions lack scale
- **Agentic AI implementation** — Shift from passive chatbots to systems that execute complex tasks autonomously
- **Structured AI adoption roadmaps** — Combine operational rigor, data readiness, and human collaboration to avoid pilot purgatory

**Weak/Risky Angles:**
- Generic "AI transformation" messaging without specific operational outcomes
- Over-emphasis on tool deployment vs. business outcomes
- Claims about ROI without proof points or case studies

### Key Messages
1. **"Stop guessing on routes. Start saving."** — Route optimization: 25% fuel savings in 90 days
2. **"Inventory that moves. Not dead stock."** — Reduce stockouts by 40% with predictive forecasting
3. **"AI that works with your legacy systems, not against them"** — Integration-first approach
4. **"From pilot to production: A structured path to AI scaling"** — Addresses the 20% scaling gap

## 4. Forms — Capture Strategy and Gates

### Field Count Impact (Critical)
| Fields | Conversion Rate | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| **1 field** | **3.20%** | Baseline low friction |
| **3 fields** | **17.0–23.1%** | Sweet spot for top-funnel |
| **5 fields** | **0.81–4.6%** | Mid-funnel; significant drop-off |
| **7+ fields** | **6.9–11.4%** | Bottom-funnel only |

### Recommended Form Strategy
| Funnel Stage | Fields | Capture |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Top-funnel** | 2–3 | Name, company, email |
| **Mid-funnel** | 5–7 | Add job title, revenue range, employee count |
| **Bottom-funnel** | 8–10 | Add timeline, budget, current solution |

### Progressive Profiling Approach
- **First touch:** 2–3 fields (name, email, company)
- **Return visitors:** Capture only missing fields via progressive profiling
- **Demo request:** 5–7 fields maximum

### Optimization Tactics
- **Multi-step forms:** Boost conversion by 14% vs. single-page forms (for 6+ fields)
- **Inline validation:** Increase completion rates by 5–13%
- **Mobile consideration:** Mobile forms convert ~32% lower than desktop

## 5. Email Sequences — Triggers, Timeline, Subject Lines

### Recommended Nurture Framework

**Trigger-Based Sequences:**
| Trigger | Duration | Email Count | Goal |
|---------|----------|-------------|------|
| Website → demo page | 10 days | 5 emails | Demo request |
| Download → checklist | 14 days | 7 emails | Education + demo |
| Webinar registration | 7 days | 5 emails | Attend + convert |
| Demo request | 3 days | 4 touches | Meeting confirmation |
| No-show demo | 10 days | 3 touches | Reschedule |

**Performance Benchmarks:**
- **Sequence length:** 4–7 emails generate 3x reply rate of shorter sequences
- **Cadence:** Day 0, 3, 7, 14, 21, 30 (increasing gaps)
- **Open rates:** Median 36–42%; top performers 50%+
- **Reply rates:** 5–10% good; 10–15% excellent
- **CTR:** Average 2–4%; top-quartile 10%+ with AI personalization

### Subject Line Best Practices
- **Length:** 6–10 words performs best
- **Style:** Specific and outcome-focused (e.g., "Reduce stockouts by 40% in 90 days")
- **Timing:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11 AM recipient timezone

### Content Strategy
- **"3:1 rule"** — Deliver value 3 times before asking for demo
- **Beyond first-name personalization:** Reference industry, previously downloaded content, recent behavior
- **Content-led nurture:** Provide frameworks, case studies, calculators vs. promotional content

## 6. Lead Rules — Scoring Signals and Thresholds

### 3-Block Lead Scoring Model

**Block 1 — FIT (Ideal Customer Profile) — 0–50 points**
- Company matches ICP (size + sector + zone): +10
- Contact is decision-maker or influencer: +15
- No blacklist (competitor, partner): +5
- Size $10M–$100M: +10
- Job title VP/Director: +10

**Block 2 — INTENT (Behavioral Signals) — 0–50 points**
- Pricing/demo visit: +15
- Downloaded ROI calculator: +20
- Attended webinar: +10
- Requested case study: +10
- Multiple page views (+5/session)

**Block 3 — TIMING (Buying Window) — 0–40 points**
- Active project with deadline: +20
- Timeline < 30 days: +25
- Recent trigger event (hiring, fundraise, migration): +15
- Budget allocated: +20

**Negative Scoring:**
- Visited only homepage: -3
- Downloaded blog post only: -5
- Job title: Individual contributor: -10
- No engagement in 14+ days: -5

### Thresholds
| Score | Classification | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **0–39** | Unqualified | Continue nurture |
| **40–59** | MQL | Nurture program |
| **60–79** | SQL | 24-hour outreach |
| **80+** | Hot lead | 1-hour response |

### LinkedIn-Specific Intent Signals
- **Content engagement:** Articles read, eBooks downloaded, webinars attended
- **Website activity:** Pricing pages, case studies, competitor comparisons
- **Social interactions:** Comments, shares, profile views (multiple in short period)
- **High-intent triggers:** Demo requests, quote requests, free trial starts
- **Dark intent:** Private community discussions, AI-driven predictive signals
